Boeing 737NG Simulator

Introduction & History

The Boeing 737 Next Generation (NG) is one of the most widely used passenger aircraft in the world. Introduced in the late 1990s, it powers short to medium-haul flights for airlines all over the globe. More than 7,000 737NGs have been built, and it remains a pilot’s workhorse for reliability and efficiency.

How It’s Used in Real Life

The 737NG is the backbone of many airlines, flying many routes such as London-Dublin or New York-Chicago daily. Pilots train extensively on full-motion flight simulators before flying passengers for the first time, making it one of the most recognized cockpits in aviation.

Simulator Experience

Step into a fully modeled Boeing 737NG flight deck. Every switch, knob, and display works just like the real aircraft. Dual-control yokes let you fly with a friend or instructor. Realistic avionics, autopilot, and airline-style checklists immerse you in the airline pilot experience.

What You Can Try

  • Fly a short-haul route and experience a full takeoff and landing.
  • Practice crosswind landings or challenging airports.
  • Try airline procedures like programming the Flight Management Computer (FMC).

Airbus A320 Simulator

Introduction & History

The Airbus A320 revolutionized aviation when it was introduced in 1988 as the world’s first fully fly-by-wire passenger jet. Known for its advanced automation and side-stick controls, it is the most popular single-aisle jet in Europe and the backbone of airlines like easyJet, Lufthansa, and Aer Lingus.

How It’s Used in Real Life

With over 10,000 A320-family aircraft flying worldwide, it carries millions of passengers every day. Pilots rely heavily on automation, but also train for manual handling in simulators just like this one.

Simulator Experience

Fly-by-wire side-stick controls and large electronic displays make this cockpit very different from the 737. Experience Airbus automation, from managed climb/descent to autoland. The simulator captures the smooth Airbus handling that pilots call “unique.”

What You Can Try

  • Learn to program the Airbus MCDU (flight computer).
  • Fly into busy airports like Heathrow or Frankfurt.
  • Experience a hands-off autoland and see how automation takes over.

Tomark Viper SD-4 Simulator

Introduction & History

The Tomark Viper SD-4 is an all-metal, two-seat low-wing light-sport aircraft developed by TomarkAero in Slovakia. Design work began in the mid-2000s, the type first flew on 1 November 2006 and was introduced to the market soon after. The Viper was built as a durable, easy-to-maintain LSA for training, touring and even glider towing.

How It’s Used in Real Life

The Viper is popular with flying clubs and private owners for primary flight training and recreational cross-country flying. It’s offered in UL/LSA variants (EASA/LSA categories) and can be equipped with modern glass avionics, ballistic parachute options and towing gear — making it versatile for clubs and schools.

Simulator Experience — what makes the Viper sim distinct

Unlike the airliner simulators, the Viper focuses on the pure basics of flight. You won’t be programming complex computers or managing heavy automation — instead, you’ll master takeoffs, landings, stalls, and navigation just like a student pilot in their very first lessons. It’s an ideal choice for beginners, aviation enthusiasts, or anyone who wants to experience the joy of small-aircraft flying before stepping into the big jets.

What You Can Try

  • First solo-style experience: pre-flight briefing → straight-forward takeoff → circuit work → landing.
  • Stall recognition & recovery: slow flight, flaps, and stall recovery practice.
  • Short/soft-field takeoffs and landings.
  • Basic navigation: pilotage & dead-reckoning cross-country legs.
  • Emergency procedures: simulated engine-out approach and emergency landing procedure.
